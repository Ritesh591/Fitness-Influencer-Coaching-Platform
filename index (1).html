What Each Table Does

User Core

Shared identity record for every person on the platform — trainers and clients alike. Role distinguishes between them. Keeping a single User table avoids duplicating name, email, and auth fields across two separate tables.

  • PK user_id
  • full_name
  • email
  • phone
  • role
  • joined_at
Trainer_Profile Core

Extended profile for users who are trainers. Stores trainer-specific fields like specialization, bio, and years of experience. Linked 1-to-1 with User so trainer data stays separate from client data without bloating the core User table.

  • PK trainer_id
  • FK user_id
  • specialization
  • bio
  • experience_years
  • instagram_handle
Client_Profile Core

Extended profile for users who are clients. Holds health baseline data — age, gender, height, initial weight, fitness goal — captured at onboarding. Separate from User so this context never pollutes auth or contact fields.

  • PK client_id
  • FK user_id
  • date_of_birth
  • gender
  • height_cm
  • initial_weight_kg
  • fitness_goal
Coaching_Plan Product

A plan or program that the trainer creates and offers — e.g., "12-Week Fat Loss", "Muscle Building Starter". Stores duration, price, plan type (workout-only, diet-only, combined), and which trainer owns it. Many clients can subscribe to the same plan.

  • PK plan_id
  • FK trainer_id
  • title
  • description
  • plan_type
  • duration_weeks
  • price
Subscription Junction

Records that a specific client enrolled in a specific plan. Tracks start date, end date, and current status (active, completed, cancelled). This is the central joining point — it links client ↔ plan and also anchors sessions, check-ins, and progress records.

  • PK subscription_id
  • FK client_id
  • FK plan_id
  • start_date
  • end_date
  • status
  • enrolled_at
Session Transaction

A scheduled live interaction — video call, consultation, or group session. Not the same as a check-in. A session has a fixed time slot, a medium (Zoom, Meet, phone), and a status (scheduled, completed, cancelled). Linked to both trainer and client.

  • PK session_id
  • FK trainer_id
  • FK client_id
  • FK subscription_id
  • scheduled_at
  • duration_min
  • medium
  • status
  • notes
CheckIn Transaction

A weekly self-report submitted by the client — not a live session. Captures how the client felt that week: energy level, sleep, diet adherence, workout completion. Separate from Session and from Progress so each concern stays clean and queryable independently.

  • PK checkin_id
  • FK subscription_id
  • FK client_id
  • submitted_at
  • energy_level
  • sleep_quality
  • diet_adherence
  • workout_completed
  • client_notes
Trainer_Feedback Operational

Trainer's written response to a client's check-in. Stored separately so feedback can be created asynchronously after the check-in is reviewed. One check-in can have at most one feedback record. This keeps trainer notes out of both the check-in and the progress table.

  • PK feedback_id
  • FK checkin_id
  • FK trainer_id
  • feedback_text
  • plan_adjustment
  • given_at
Progress_Entry Analytics

Objective measurement record — weight, body fat %, and key body measurements logged over time. One entry per measurement date per client. Kept separate from check-ins (which are subjective) and from the client profile (which holds only the initial baseline).

  • PK progress_id
  • FK client_id
  • FK subscription_id
  • recorded_at
  • weight_kg
  • body_fat_pct
  • chest_cm
  • waist_cm
  • hips_cm
Payment Financial

Records each payment transaction tied to a subscription. A subscription may have multiple payments if it's a recurring billing model. Stores amount, method (UPI, card, bank transfer), and payment status. Keeps financial data fully separate from plan and client records.

  • PK payment_id
  • FK subscription_id
  • FK client_id
  • amount
  • payment_method
  • payment_status
  • paid_at
Diet_Plan Content

A custom diet plan created by a trainer for a specific client under a subscription. Stores calorie targets, macro breakdown, and meal timing notes. Separate from Coaching_Plan (which is the product) so that nutrition content can be updated without changing the subscription record.

  • PK diet_plan_id
  • FK subscription_id
  • FK trainer_id
  • title
  • daily_calories
  • protein_g
  • carbs_g
  • fats_g
  • notes
  • created_at
Workout_Plan Content

A custom workout routine assigned to a client under a subscription. Stores the training split, frequency, and any special instructions. Kept separate from Diet_Plan and Coaching_Plan so workout content can evolve independently across the subscription period.

  • PK workout_plan_id
  • FK subscription_id
  • FK trainer_id
  • title
  • training_split
  • days_per_week
  • notes
  • created_at

Business Questions Answered by This Design

1

Who are the trainers and who are the clients?

Both live in the USER table, differentiated by the role column. Trainers have a corresponding TRAINER_PROFILE row with their specialization and bio; clients have a CLIENT_PROFILE row with their health baseline. This single-table-with-profiles pattern avoids duplicating name and email fields.

2

What coaching programs exist and which trainer owns them?

COACHING_PLAN stores all programs. Each plan has a trainer_id FK linking it to its creator via TRAINER_PROFILE. Filter by trainer_id to see all plans a specific trainer offers. The plan_type column distinguishes workout-only, diet-only, or combined programs.

3

Which client purchased which plan, and when does it start and end?

SUBSCRIPTION is the central junction. It holds client_id FK and plan_id FK alongside start_date, end_date, and status. Query subscriptions filtered by client_id to see all plans a client has ever purchased, including past and active ones.

4

Can multiple clients enroll in the same plan? Can one trainer handle many clients?

Yes on both. COACHING_PLAN to SUBSCRIPTION is one-to-many — many clients can subscribe to the same plan. TRAINER_PROFILE to COACHING_PLAN is also one-to-many — one trainer creates many plans, and through those plans handles many clients.

5

Are consultations or live sessions being scheduled?

SESSION records every scheduled interaction. It links to both trainer_id and client_id, stores the scheduled_at timestamp, medium (Zoom, Meet, phone), and status. A session is optionally tied to a subscription_id — some sessions (standalone consultations) may exist without a long-term plan.

6

Are clients submitting weekly check-ins? How is that different from sessions?

Check-ins are asynchronous self-reports in CHECKIN — the client fills in energy, sleep, diet adherence, and notes on their own schedule. Sessions are scheduled live calls in SESSION. They are deliberately separate tables so you can independently count how many check-ins a client submitted vs. how many live calls they attended.

7

How are trainer notes and feedback stored?

TRAINER_FEEDBACK is linked to a specific checkin_id and trainer_id. This means feedback is a response to a check-in, created asynchronously. It stores feedback_text and a plan_adjustment note. Keeping it separate means the trainer can review the check-in first, then write feedback without locking the check-in record.

8

How is client progress tracked over time?

PROGRESS_ENTRY stores objective measurements — weight, body fat %, and body measurements — each as a separate dated row. This is separate from CLIENT_PROFILE (which holds only the initial baseline) and from CHECKIN (which holds subjective weekly feelings). Query progress entries by client_id ordered by recorded_at to plot a progress chart.

9

How are diet plans and workout plans modeled?

DIET_PLAN and WORKOUT_PLAN are separate content tables, each linked to a subscription_id. This means a trainer can update a client's nutrition guidance or training split mid-subscription without touching the subscription record or the coaching plan template. A combined-type subscription can have both; a diet-only subscription has only a diet plan.

10

How is payment and subscription billing stored?

PAYMENT links to subscription_id and client_id. A subscription can have multiple payment rows (for monthly billing cycles). Each payment records amount, payment_method (UPI, card, bank transfer), and payment_status (pending, paid, refunded). Financial history is fully preserved since payments are never overwritten.
